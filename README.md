# CyberInfiniti-Governance-Risk-Compliance
This project establishes a resilient, ISO-aligned governance and compliance framework to strengthen CyberInfiniti Ltd’s enterprise security posture and regulatory readiness.
It involves the design and implementation of an ISO/IEC 27002:2022-aligned enterprise information security governance and compliance framework, built on structured policy development, risk-based controls, and measurable compliance monitoring. The initiative ensures resilience, accountability, and continuous improvement, while positioning CyberInfiniti Ltd for regulatory compliance and certification readiness.

# Project Overview
This project establishes a resilient, ISO/IEC 27002:2022-aligned governance and compliance framework to strengthen Cyberinfiniti Ltd’s enterprise security posture and regulatory readiness.
Through a comprehensive Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) initiative, Cyberinfiniti Ltd transitioned from an informal, ad-hoc security structure into a structured, policy-driven, and measurable compliance ecosystem. The framework is aligned with:
- ISO/IEC 27002:2022 – for control implementation and best practices
- Nigeria Data Protection Act (NDPA) 2023 – ensuring national regulatory compliance
- Client contractual obligations – meeting bespoke security requirements
- SOC 2 security principles – reinforcing trust and service assurance
- ISO/IEC 27001 certification readiness – preparing for formal certification
  
This initiative demonstrates practical implementation of enterprise policy development, compliance monitoring, executive governance, and regulatory alignment within a cybersecurity consulting and managed security services organization. It highlights Cyberinfiniti Ltd’s commitment to resilience, accountability, and continuous improvement in information security governance.

# Three (3) Strategic Policies that aligns with our organization (Operational Core)

## 1. Third-Party & Vendor Risk Management
**Why Critical?**  
Cybersecurity firms like Cyberinfiniti rely heavily on external vendors (e.g., cloud providers, SIEM platforms, EDR solutions). With the rise of **supply chain attacks** and the NDPA’s requirement for **processor due diligence**, vendor risk governance is essential to protect sensitive client data and maintain regulatory compliance.  

### Control implemented: Third‑Party & Vendor Risk Management

- **Pre‑engagement security assessments** – evaluate vendor posture before onboarding  
- **Risk‑based onboarding** – tailor due diligence to vendor criticality  
- **Contractual security clauses** – embed compliance and security obligations in agreements  
- **NDA requirement** – enforce confidentiality for all vendors handling sensitive data  
- **Annual vendor reassessment** – ensure ongoing compliance and resilience  
- **Right‑to‑audit clause** – grant oversight authority for verification  
- **Termination security controls** – safeguard data and access upon contract closure

## 2. Vulnerability Management & Secure Testing  

**Why Critical?**  
As a cybersecurity firm, Cyberinfiniti must **practice what it preaches**. Effective vulnerability management and secure testing are essential to maintain credibility, protect client environments, and comply with both ISO/IEC 27002 and NDPA requirements.  

### Controls Implemented: Vunerability Management & Secure Testing 
- **Monthly internal scans** – continuous detection of weaknesses within the environment  
- **Quarterly external scans** – independent validation of perimeter defenses  
- **CVSS‑based prioritization** – risk‑driven remediation aligned with industry standards  
- **Critical remediation within 7 days** – rapid response to high‑severity findings  
- **Formal exception management** – documented approval for deferred fixes  
- **Annual penetration testing** – proactive identification of exploitable flaws  
- **Change management alignment** – ensuring vulnerabilities are addressed during system updates  
- **Central vulnerability register** – consolidated tracking of issues and resolutions  
- **Executive dashboard reporting** – transparent oversight for leadership and auditors  

## 3. Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery  

**Why Critical?**  
If **SOC operations fail, clients suffer**. Business continuity and disaster recovery (BC/DR) are essential to safeguard client trust, maintain regulatory compliance, and ensure resilience against operational disruptions.  

### Controls Implemented: Business Continuity & Disaster Recovery  
- **Annual Business Impact Analysis (BIA)** – identify critical processes and dependencies  
- **Defined RTO and RPO** – establish recovery time and recovery point objectives  
- **Encrypted geographically separate backups** – protect data integrity and availability  
- **Immutable backup strategy** – prevent tampering and ensure reliability  
- **Bi‑annual restoration testing** – validate recovery effectiveness under real conditions  
- **Executive crisis escalation** – ensure leadership oversight during incidents  
- **Annual BC/DR simulation exercises** – test organizational readiness and response capability
